Getting a blood test is step one. Knowing what your results mean — and tracking how they change over time — is where the real value lives. A single blood test is a snapshot. A series of tests tracked over months and years is a health trajectory.

The problem is that most people get their results as a PDF, glance at the flags, and file it away. No trending, no context, no connection to lifestyle data. Blood test tracking apps solve this by extracting your biomarker values, comparing them to reference ranges, and showing how each marker moves over time.

Here are the best options available in 2026, what each does well, and where each falls short.

## What to look for in a blood test tracking app

Before comparing specific apps, these are the features that matter most:

**PDF upload and auto-extraction** — manually typing in 30+ biomarker values is tedious and error-prone. The best apps accept a PDF or photo of your lab report and extract values automatically using OCR or AI.

**Lab-agnostic** — some apps only work with their own testing service. If you already get blood work through your doctor, Quest, Labcorp, or any other lab, you want an app that accepts results from any source.

**Trend tracking** — seeing your current results is useful. Seeing how they have changed over the last 2 years is transformative. Charts, timelines, and historical comparisons are essential.

**Reference ranges and optimal ranges** — most apps show standard lab reference ranges. The best ones also show optimal ranges used by longevity-focused physicians, which are often tighter than standard ranges.

**Wearable integration** — blood biomarkers in isolation miss half the picture. Combining lab data with Apple Watch, Oura, or Garmin data (sleep, HRV, resting heart rate, activity) creates a much richer health view.

**Privacy and data control** — your blood test data is among the most sensitive personal health information. Look for apps that store data locally or use encrypted cloud storage with clear privacy policies.

### 2. InsideTracker

**Best for**: bundled blood testing and tracking with DNA integration

InsideTracker is a testing-and-tracking platform — you order blood tests through them (drawn at Quest locations or via mobile phlebotomy), and results appear in their dashboard with personalized recommendations. Their Ultimate plan tests up to 48 biomarkers. They also offer DNA testing that analyzes genetic markers related to fitness, inflammation, and nutrient metabolism, and integrate with wearables like Garmin and Apple Health.

**Key features**:
- Blood testing at Quest locations or at-home mobile draw
- Up to 48 biomarkers with AI-driven recommendations
- DNA integration (20,000+ genetic markers)
- Wearable sync (Garmin, Apple Health, Fitbit)
- InnerAge biological age calculation

**Pricing**: $149/year membership plus per-test costs ($199 for Foundation/26 markers, $589 for Ultimate/48 markers)

**Limitations**: expensive when you add testing costs; only tracks results from their own panels — you cannot upload results from your doctor or other labs; US and Canada only

### 3. Function Health

**Best for**: comprehensive testing with 100+ biomarkers in a single panel

Function Health offers one of the broadest testing panels available — over 100 biomarkers covering metabolic health, cardiovascular markers (including ApoB and Lp(a)), liver, kidney, thyroid, hormones, nutrients, heavy metals, inflammatory markers, and autoimmune markers. The annual membership includes two lab visits at Quest Diagnostics.

**Key features**:
- 100+ biomarkers per panel (the most comprehensive standard panel available)
- Two lab visits per year included in membership
- Dashboard with trend tracking across visits
- Includes advanced markers like Lp(a), ApoB, cystatin C, and heavy metals that most doctors do not order

**Pricing**: $499/year (includes two full panels)

**Limitations**: only tracks results from their own panels — no external PDF upload; requires Quest Diagnostics visits (no at-home option); no wearable integration; US only; high price point

### 4. SiPhox Health

**Best for**: at-home finger-prick testing with wearable integration

SiPhox uses silicon photonic technology for at-home blood collection via finger prick — no lab visit required. They offer five specialized panels ranging from 18 to 60+ biomarkers. The platform integrates with over 300 devices including Apple Watch, Oura Ring, CGMs, and Eight Sleep.

**Key features**:
- At-home finger-prick collection (no lab visit needed)
- 5 specialized panels (Core, Heart, Hormone, etc.)
- Integration with 300+ devices and wearables
- Longitudinal tracking with health score

**Pricing**: panels start at $124 per test; subscription plans reduce per-test cost (monthly plans from ~$24/month)

**Limitations**: finger-prick collection means smaller sample volumes and potentially fewer markers per panel; some advanced markers require venous blood draw; newer company with less track record

### 5. Carrot Care

**Best for**: free bloodwork tracking on iPhone with biological age calculation

Carrot Care is an iPhone app that lets you upload lab results via PDF or photo and tracks 1,000+ biomarkers with reference ranges. It includes a phenotypic age calculator that estimates biological age from your bloodwork. Data is stored locally on your device with optional iCloud backup.

**Key features**:
- PDF and photo upload with OCR extraction
- 1,000+ biomarker database
- Phenotypic age calculator
- Local data storage (privacy-first)
- Free core features

**Pricing**: free tier available; premium tier for enhanced OCR accuracy and additional features

**Limitations**: iPhone only; no wearable integration; no optimal/longevity ranges; OCR accuracy can vary by lab format; premium features require subscription

### 6. Soka

**Best for**: organizing multiple types of medical tests in one platform

Soka is a medical test management platform that goes beyond blood tests — it supports hair mineral analysis, urine tests, and other lab work. AI reads your reports and extracts values automatically. The interface includes heatmaps, interactive timelines, and side-by-side test comparisons. Data is processed and stored in the EU under GDPR compliance.

**Key features**:
- Supports multiple test types (blood, urine, hair mineral analysis)
- AI-powered PDF extraction
- Interactive timelines and heatmaps
- GDPR-compliant EU data storage
- Side-by-side test comparison

**Pricing**: free tier with limited features; paid plans for full access

**Limitations**: more of a medical records organizer than a health optimization tool; no wearable integration; no optimal ranges for longevity; web-based (no native mobile app)

### 7. Biotracker

**Best for**: free, privacy-first blood test tracking with no account required

Biotracker is a free web-based tool from Australia. Drag and drop your lab PDF, and it extracts biomarkers and displays them in clean interactive charts. All processing happens client-side in your browser — no data is sent to a server. You can export reports to share with your doctor.

**Key features**:
- Completely free with no account required
- Client-side processing (data never leaves your browser)
- PDF drag-and-drop with auto-extraction
- Interactive charts and trend visualization
- Export reports for your doctor

**Pricing**: free

**Limitations**: web-only (no mobile app); limited biomarker database compared to dedicated platforms; no reference range context or health insights; no wearable integration; basic feature set

### 8. Apple Health (Health Records)

**Best for**: passively receiving lab results from supported healthcare providers

If your healthcare provider supports Apple Health Records, your lab results can flow directly into the Health app on your iPhone. This is not a dedicated blood test tracker — it is a feature within Apple's health ecosystem. Results appear alongside your other health data (steps, heart rate, sleep).

**Key features**:
- Automatic sync from supported healthcare providers
- Integrated with Apple Watch data natively
- No manual upload required
- Strong privacy (on-device encryption)

**Pricing**: free (built into iPhone)

**Limitations**: only works if your healthcare provider supports Health Records (many do not); no PDF upload for results from other labs; no trend visualization or biomarker analysis; no reference range context beyond what the provider sends; no optimal ranges
